The Mandate

HR is a business function — measured, proactive, owned.

In a company that ships games into regulated casino markets, people are the entire product. The HR Manager exists to make sure the company can hire, grow, keep, and protect the talent that builds and sells those games — and to do it on a visible cadence with numbers attached. The role is not reactive paperwork. It owns outcomes.

DOES What this role owns

  • Whether critical roles are filled fast and well
  • Whether new hires are productive and stay past 90 days
  • Whether people have goals, feedback, and a path to grow
  • Whether pay is fair, competitive, and defensible
  • Whether the company is engaged, healthy, and not burning out on release crunches
  • Whether the company is legally and license-compliant on people matters
  • Whether leadership has accurate people data to decide with

NOT What this role is not

  • Not "the person who does contracts when asked"
  • Not waiting for problems to walk in the door
  • Not invisible between hiring requests
  • Not optional reporting — every function carries a metric
  • Not a cost centre with no targets
  • Not separate from the release roadmap and commercial goals

RUN Operating principles

P1Proactive, not reactive. The calendar drives the work — surveys, reviews, planning and reporting happen on schedule whether or not anyone asks.
P2Everything is measured. Each function below has KPIs. If it isn't tracked, it isn't managed.
P3Tied to the roadmap. Hiring and capacity plans follow the game-release schedule and commercial targets, not the other way around.
P4Visible to leadership. A monthly people dashboard reaches the leadership team without being requested.
P5Builds to scale. Every people system — hiring, onboarding, reviews, records — is documented and repeatable, so it runs the same way as the company grows instead of living in one person's head.
